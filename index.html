I'm interested in computational systems that learn from data how to solve real problems. A major problem is human communication: although technology has broken down communication barriers of all kinds all over the world, the biggest barrier remains the fact that humans speak many different languages. My research focuses on technologies that will help overcome this barrier. I focus on algorithms and models for statistical natural language processing systems, and I draw on diverse ideas from (mostly) computer science to build these systems.

I am a research fellow in the machine translation research group at the University of Edinburgh. I earned my Ph.D. in computer science at the University of Maryland. I've had the good fortune to collaborate with many excellent researchers, including Abhishek Arun, Michael Auli, Phil Blunsom, David Chiang, Chris Dyer, Juri Ganitkevitch, Barry Haddow, Hieu Hoang, Rebecca Hwa, Philipp Koehn, Nitin Madnani, Christof Monz, Philip Resnik, and Jonny Weese.
