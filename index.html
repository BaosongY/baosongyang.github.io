Adam Lopez

I build computational systems that learn from data how to solve real-world problems. A major problem arises in human communication: although technology has broken down communication barriers of all kinds all over the world, the biggest barrier is the fact that we speak many different languages. My research and teaching focus on technology that will break this barrier, namely machine translation systems that learn how to translate from vast amounts of language data (like Google Translate). Improvements to these systems depend on the extension and application of ideas from diverse fields such as algorithms, machine learning, formal language and automata theory, and computational linguistics. I am interested in variety of problems in these fields and others.

I am a research scientist in the Human Language Technology Center of Excellence (HLTCOE) at Johns Hopkins University. Previously I was a research fellow in the machine translation research group at the University of Edinburgh, where I moved after earning my Ph.D. in computer science from the University of Maryland. I've had the good fortune to collaborate with many excellent researchers, including Abhishek Arun, Michael Auli, Phil Blunsom, David Chiang, Chris Dyer, Juri Ganitkevitch, Barry Haddow, Hieu Hoang, Rebecca Hwa, Philipp Koehn, Nitin Madnani, Christof Monz, and Philip Resnik.
