Other Teaching Material

I have taught extensively on machine translation, giving tutorial lectures and invited short courses at many summer schools in North America and Europe. Over time I developed a substantial amount of teaching material. Many people have reused this material, often improving it. You are welcome to reuse or study from it as well.

  • I wrote a survey paper on statistical machine translation that appeared in ACM Computing Surveys in 2008. It gives a comprehensive overview of the field, aimed at first-year graduate students. Although much has happened since it was published, most of the concepts in it remain vital, and the paper is widely cited.
  • I taught a graduate course on machine translation. The course page contains all of my lectures, which include the material from my short courses and much more. They are freely reusable under a Creative Commons license.
  • I developed a set of open-ended challenge problems for students learning about machine translation. They are simple, self-contained, and concise. Get the code on github. Within minutes you can experiment with basic algorithms of statistical machine translation, hone your knowledge of textbook algorithms by implementing them, or prototype new algorithms and evaluate them. Try it out!
